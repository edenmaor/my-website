My Hobbies

probably better than yours

I'm a former senior theoretical particle physicist at the California Institute of Technology, focusing on string theory and its alter ego M-theory. I held this position for three-and-a-half years
and then promoted to junior professor in order to change my field of study to dark matter,
with the stipulation that i'd teach a graduate-level class on analytical mechanics.
For my contributions to theoretical physics,I won a Caltech Chancellor's Award for Science.

After focusing for many years in string theory and practical physics, I pivoted to the dark matter field.

Lately i worked with my girlfriend Amy on a a Super-Asymmetry for string theory which was published and widely applauded.
in 2019 we won the noble Prizes in Physics for the development of Super Asymmetry.
Can say without a doubt that it was my happiest moment ever.
